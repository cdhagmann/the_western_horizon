Appendix E: Resources & Further Reading

The Western Horizon builds on the West Marches tradition and collaborative worldbuilding practices. These resources provide essential context and additional guidance.

West Marches Essentials

Required Reading

Before running The Western Horizon, read these foundational articles about the West Marches campaign style:

What is West Marches?

Link: https://www.westmarches.games/guide/what-is-west-marches

This article explains the core principles of West Marches play:

  • Player-driven exploration: Players organize sessions and choose destinations
  • Open table: Flexible roster, any combination of players per session
  • Shared world: Persistent setting with cumulative discovery
  • The Guild model: Central hub where adventurers gather
  • Session reports: How discoveries become common knowledge

Running West Marches

Link: https://www.westmarches.games/guide/running-west-marches

Practical advice for DMs running West Marches campaigns:

  • Scheduling: How to manage an open table with rotating players
  • Prep strategies: Preparing for player-driven content
  • The bulletin board: Quest hooks and rumors
  • Danger levels: Telegraphing risk and tier-appropriate content
  • Common pitfalls: What to avoid and how to recover
